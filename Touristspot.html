PAOAY CHURCH

Paoay Church, officially named the Saint Augustine Church of Paoay, is a historic
treasure located in Paoay, Ilocos Norte, Philippines. It's recognized for its Baroque
architecture with massive coral stone buttresses. Built in the 18th century, this
architectural masterpiece was completed in 1710. Paoay Church is celebrated for its unique design, fusing European Baroque, Gothic, and Oriental elements. It's famous
for its wooden altars and decorative interiors.

Notably, the church, along with three others, was designated a UNESCO World
Heritage Site in 1993 under the title "Baroque Churches of the Philippines." This
recognition underscores its historical and architectural importance.

Paoay Church draws visitors worldwide due to its cultural significance and
picturesque setting, making it a must-visit attraction for those interested in history
and architecture.

PAOAY SAND DUNES

Located near Paoay, Ilocos Norte, Philippines, the Paoay Sand
Dunes form a unique, ever-changing landscape sculpted by
wind and tide.Thrill-seekers revel in sandboarding and 4x4 adventures.

As the sun sets, the dunes transform into a golden masterpiece.
These dunes have also graced the silver screen and remain a
must-visit adventure destination.

PAOAY LAKE

Located near Paoay in Ilocos Norte, Philippines, Paoay Lake offers a tranquil escape surrounded by lush landscapes. Boating is a favored pastime, allowing visitors to peacefully traverse the calm waters. The lake's picturesque beauty is a delight
for nature enthusiasts and photographers, particularly during sunsets.

Moreover, Paoay Lake is a haven for birdwatchers, attracting a diverse range of avian
species. Accessible from Paoay's town center, this natural sanctuary is a convenient
getaway for those seeking serenity and a reconnection with nature. It's more than
just a body of water; it's a place where urban life's noise fades away, replaced by the
calming rhythms of the natural world. Whether you're gently rowing on its waters,
capturing reflections, birdwatching, or simply savoring its beauty, Paoay Lake invites
you to rediscover the tranquility of nature.

MALACANANG OF THE NORTH

Located in Paoay, Ilocos Norte, the Malacañang of the North
is a historical mansion with an iconic past. It once served as the
official residence of President Ferdinand Marcos and
his family during his presidency, making it a place where
significant political decisions were made.

The mansion's architecture is a blend of modernist and Balinese
styles, reflecting the opulent lifestyle of the Marcos family during
their rule. Today, it has been transformed into a museum and cultural
center, showcasing memorabilia from the Marcos era, offering visitors
insights into the nation's political history.

Its picturesque location next to Paoay Lake adds to the mansion's appeal,
providing serene lakeside views. This historic site serves as an educational
destination, allowing visitors to delve into the Philippines' political history.

The Malacañang of the North is easily accessible, making it a must-visit for those
interested in Philippine history and culture. It stands as a living record of the nation's political past, reminding us of the importance of understanding history to shape a better future.
